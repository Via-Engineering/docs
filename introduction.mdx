Finding the right electronic component has always meant juggling distributor searches, half-populated databases, and stacks of PDFs. Via BOM changes that by reading every datasheet end-to-end — not just indexing a curated subset of fields — so you get the full picture on any part before you commit it to your BOM. Whether you're hunting for a specific part number or describing a requirement in plain English, Via BOM brings the answer back with the context you need to make a confident decision.

## What Via BOM Does

Via BOM is built around a single workflow: search for a part, inspect its details, and add it to your BOM. Every step of that workflow is AI-assisted.

When you search, you can type anything: a part number like `INA228`, a spec fragment like `low noise 3.3V LDO`, or a full plain-language requirement like `overvoltage protection IC for a 12V automotive rail`. Via BOM interprets your query, applies any active project context, and returns a ranked shortlist. Each result comes with AI-generated tradeoff explanations — not just a score, but a plain-English summary of why one part outranks another for your specific constraints.

When you open a part, you see specs extracted directly from the datasheet: electrical parameters, package information, operating conditions, and more. Via BOM links to the source datasheet and surfaces nearby series variants so you can evaluate alternatives without starting a new search. Once you've found the right part, you add it to your project BOM in a single action.

## Who It's For

Via BOM is built for hardware engineers and PCB designers who need to move from "find the right part" to "commit it to the BOM" without bouncing between a distributor site, a datasheet PDF, and a spreadsheet. It's equally useful for engineering leads who want to encode preferred parts and supply-chain rules once and have those preferences enforced across an entire team's searches. Procurement and supply chain roles benefit from the shared workspace model, which keeps approved vendor lists, lifecycle constraints, and cost targets in sync across every project the team works on.
