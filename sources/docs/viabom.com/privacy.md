# Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 9, 2026

## 1\. Introduction

Via Engineering Inc. ("Via Engineering," "Company," "we," "us," or "our") provides Via BOM, an AI-assisted electronic component search, BOM, project, library, ruleset, API, and MCP integration platform (collectively, the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you use the Service.

**Effective Date:** June 9, 2026

By using the Service, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not access or use the Service.

## 2\. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information You Provide

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when you interact with the Service, including:

- **Account and Workspace Information:** Your name, email address, profile picture, organization, role, invitation status, and related authentication or organization membership information. Authentication is handled through WorkOS AuthKit and connected identity providers.
- **Workspace Content:** Projects, BOMs, revisions, components, quantities, notes, libraries, rulesets, policy settings, folders, saved searches, imports, and other data you create, upload, paste, or save in the Service.
- **Search and Engineering Inputs:** Search queries, filters, distributor selections, BOM context, rule text, component selections, feedback on search results, and other inputs you submit to search, AI, MCP, API, or import features.
- **Communications and Feedback:** Waitlist submissions, support requests, bug reports, session replay links you choose to attach, and other messages or feedback you send to us.

### 2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we automatically collect certain information, including:

- **Usage Data:** Feature usage, page views, search interactions, component views, import attempts, add-to-BOM actions, MCP/API activity, and similar product events.
- **Search Telemetry:** Search queries, normalized query hashes, timing metrics, result counts, top-result summaries, selected distributor, planner/model metadata, policy context summaries, and related diagnostics. Search telemetry may store hashed authentication identifiers and hashed IP addresses.
- **MCP and API Request Data:** MCP and API requests may generate authentication records, request metadata, tool names, tool arguments, tool results, error logs, and search telemetry when a tool or API call runs search, sourcing, BOM, library, ruleset, or project operations.
- **Device, Browser, and Log Data:** IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, referring URLs, access times, server logs, error reports, and performance information.
- **Cookies, Local Storage, and Similar Technologies:** We use essential session cookies, preference storage, and analytics technologies. See Section 7 for details.

### 2.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information from third-party sources, including:

- **Authentication Providers:** WorkOS and connected identity providers may provide profile, organization, invitation, and membership information authorized by you or your organization.
- **Component Data Providers:** Manufacturers, distributors, datasheet sources, and supply-chain providers may provide component specifications, pricing, stock, lead time, links, and related technical data.
- **Service Providers:** Analytics, hosting, database, email, AI/model, embedding, search, and infrastructure providers may provide operational logs, delivery status, usage reports, or diagnostic information.

## 3\. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

- **Providing the Service:** To authenticate users, manage organizations and workspaces, parse BOM imports, run component search, display distributor data, maintain projects, libraries, rulesets, and BOMs, and support MCP/API integrations.
- **AI-Assisted Features:** To process search queries, rule text, BOM context, component summaries, top-result payloads, and similar engineering inputs through AI, embedding, and search providers when needed to provide AI-assisted search, ranking, summaries, narrative explanations, rule parsing, and recommendations.
- **MCP and API Integrations:** To authenticate connected clients, route tool calls, execute requested operations, return results to the client, and debug or secure those integrations.
- **Personalization and Preferences:** To remember your workspace, interface, table, sidebar, theme, cookie-banner, and search preferences.
- **Analytics, Debugging, and Product Improvement:** To understand usage, diagnose errors, measure performance, evaluate search quality, improve ranking and workflows, and keep the Service reliable.
- **Communications:** To send you service-related notices, security alerts, account updates, and, where permitted, promotional communications about new features or offerings.
- **Security and Fraud Prevention:** To detect, prevent, and respond to security incidents, fraud, abuse, and violations of our Terms of Service.
- **Legal Compliance:** To comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, and government requests.

We do not use your private workspace content to train AI models. Third-party AI providers process prompts, context, and outputs as needed to provide AI-assisted features under their own terms and our provider settings; where provider controls are available, we configure them to disable model training on customer content. We may use aggregated or de-identified metrics, evaluations, and feedback to improve the Service.

## 4\. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell or share your personal information as those terms are defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA"). We may disclose information in the following circumstances:

### 4.1 Service Providers

We share information with vendors who help us provide the Service, including hosting and edge infrastructure, databases, authentication, analytics, email delivery, AI/model providers, embedding providers, vector/search infrastructure, rate limiting, error monitoring, and customer support. We may maintain a separate subprocessor or service-provider list and update it as vendors change.

### 4.2 Organization and Collaboration Features

If you use the Service through an organization or shared workspace, your organization administrators and teammates may be able to access workspace content, projects, BOMs, libraries, rulesets, saved searches, and membership information according to the product's permission model.

### 4.3 MCP and API Clients

If you connect the Service to a third-party MCP or API client, such as an IDE, assistant, automation, or CLI, information may be sent between Via BOM and that client so the requested tool or API operation can run. The third-party client may independently collect, store, or transmit information under its own terms and privacy policy.

### 4.4 Component, Distributor, and Manufacturer Services

When you request pricing, stock, datasheet, sourcing, or distributor-specific information, we may query third-party component, manufacturer, distributor, or supply-chain services and display data or links from those services. Their websites and APIs are governed by their own terms and privacy policies. This data, including datasheets, pricing, and stock information, is provided for your convenience, may be derived or inferred rather than sourced directly, may be affected by our automated extraction and processing, and may be inaccurate or out of date. Your use of such third-party data and materials is subject to the source providers' own terms, and our Terms of Service describe these limitations and how to request removal of hosted content.

### 4.5 Business Transfers

If Via Engineering is involved in a merger, acquisition, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change and any choices you may have regarding your information.

### 4.6 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law, or if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, protect our rights or property, prevent fraud, or protect the safety of our users or the public.

### 4.7 With Your Consent

We may share your information for purposes not described in this Privacy Policy with your explicit consent.

### 4.8 Aggregated and De-Identified Data

We may share aggregated, anonymized, or de-identified data that cannot reasonably be used to identify you. This may include aggregated component search trends, usage statistics, performance metrics, or market insights.

## 5\. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the Service, maintain security, debug issues, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, and support legitimate business purposes. Unless a different period is required by law, contract, backup systems, or operational needs, our current retention targets are:

- **Account, Organization, and Workspace Content:** For the duration of the account or organization relationship, and generally up to 30 days after verified deletion or termination from active systems.
- **Search Telemetry and Analytics:** Up to 24 months in identifiable or pseudonymous form, then aggregated, de-identified, or deleted where practical.
- **Operational Logs and Error Reports:** Generally up to 90 days.
- **Backups:** Generally up to 35 days before rotation or overwrite.
- **Aggregated or De-Identified Data:** May be retained longer because it is not reasonably used to identify you.

You may request deletion of personal information as described in Section 8. Some information may remain in backups, logs, aggregate data, or records we are required or permitted to keep.

## 6\. Data Security

We implement technical and organizational measures designed to protect your information, including encryption in transit, access controls, credential separation, and operational monitoring. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If we become aware of a security breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you without undue delay and, where required, within 72 hours or another timeframe required by applicable law.

## 7\. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

### 7.1 Types of Cookies We Use

- **Essential Cookies and Tokens:** Required for authentication, session management, security, and API access. These include server session cookies and, in some environments, bearer tokens used by the app or MCP/CLI clients.
- **Functional Storage:** Local storage and cookies that remember settings such as cookie-banner acceptance, theme, sidebar state, split-screen state, table preferences, invitation flow state, and temporary development or testing state.
- **Analytics and Session Diagnostics:** PostHog and related tooling help us understand product usage, diagnose issues, and, where enabled, link bug reports to session replay URLs.
- **Performance and Error Diagnostics:** Logs and telemetry help us identify slow searches, failed requests, and reliability issues.

### 7.2 Your Cookie Choices

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies and local storage through their settings. You can typically refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or clear local storage. Note that disabling essential cookies, local storage, or similar technologies may prevent the Service from functioning properly.

### 7.3 Do Not Track and Opt-Out Preference Signals

The Service does not currently respond to "Do Not Track" browser signals due to the lack of an industry standard for this feature. If we receive a legally recognized opt-out preference signal, such as Global Privacy Control, and we engage in activity that constitutes a sale or sharing of personal information under applicable law, we will treat that signal as a request to opt out for the browser, device, or account where required.

## 8\. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

- **Access:** Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
- **Correction:** Request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
- **Deletion:** Request that we delete your personal information, subject to certain legal exceptions.
- **Portability:** Request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Opt-Out of Marketing:** Unsubscribe from promotional emails by clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any marketing email or by updating your communication preferences in your account settings.
- **Withdraw Consent:** Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
- **Restrict Processing:** Request that we limit how we use your information in certain circumstances.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@viabom.com. We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by applicable law.

## 9\. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act and the California Privacy Rights Act, including:

- The right to know what personal information we collect, use, and disclose.
- The right to delete your personal information.
- The right to opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information. Via Engineering does not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined under the CCPA.
- The right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights.
- The right to correct inaccurate personal information.
- The right to limit the use of sensitive personal information.
- The right to use an opt-out preference signal, such as Global Privacy Control, where required by law.

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@viabom.com or use the mechanisms provided in Section 8.
