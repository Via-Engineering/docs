In Via BOM, workspaces are the organization-level container for all shared work. Your active workspace determines which projects, libraries, rulesets, and settings you see — and which ones your teammates can access. Everything in Via operates within the context of a workspace, so knowing which one is active is the first thing to check when something looks off.

## What a Workspace Controls

The active workspace determines which shared items you're working with:

- **Projects** — BOMs and search sessions scoped to a program or team effort
- **Libraries** — saved components your team has approved or standardized on
- **Rulesets** — supply-chain and sourcing policies that shape search ranking
- **Workspace settings and membership** — who belongs to the organization and how it's configured

<Tip>
If a project or ruleset seems to have disappeared, check the active workspace first — you may be in a different organization than expected.
</Tip>

## Switching Workspaces

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the workspace selector">
    Click the workspace selector at the top of the main sidebar. This shows your current organization and opens the switcher menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the organization you want">
    Choose the workspace you want to switch to. Via will update your view to reflect that organization's projects, libraries, rulesets, and settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The same workspace selector menu is also where you can:

- **Search** your available workspaces by name
- **Create** a new workspace
- **Join** an existing workspace you've been invited to
- **Open workspace settings** for the current organization

## Creating and Joining

**Create a new workspace** when you need a separate shared environment for a distinct team, customer engagement, or product line. Each workspace keeps its projects, libraries, and rulesets isolated from other workspaces, so this is the right move when you want a clean boundary.

**Join an existing workspace** when someone on a team has invited you. After accepting, the workspace will appear in your selector so you can switch to it.

<Note>
If the right workspace isn't showing up after accepting an invitation, confirm the invitation was sent to the same email address you use to sign in. Invitations sent to a secondary or alias address won't automatically connect to a different login.
</Note>

## Shared vs. Personal

Workspaces are designed for shared context, not just personal organization. That means other members of the workspace can see and reuse the projects, libraries, and rulesets that live there, subject to the product's current permissions model.

This is intentional — the value of a workspace comes from your team working with a consistent shared foundation. If you're seeing colleagues' projects or rulesets, that's expected behavior, not unexpected exposure.

## Workspace Settings

Workspace settings cover organization-level setup that applies across all members and projects in the workspace:

- **Profile and branding** — the workspace name, logo, and display details
- **Members and invitations** — who belongs to the workspace and pending invite management
- **Domain-related settings** — any domain restrictions or automatic join rules for your organization

You generally don't need these settings for day-to-day part work, but they're important when setting up a new team or onboarding a larger group.

## A Note on Workspace Awareness

<Note>
Workspace behavior is central to everything in Via BOM. Before filing a bug report about missing data — projects, libraries, rulesets, or search history — always confirm you're in the right workspace and include the workspace name in your report. Most cases of "missing" data turn out to be a workspace mismatch rather than a product issue.
</Note>
